I am a Lecturer (Assistant Professor) in the School of Electronics and Computer Science at the University of Southampton . I mainly research generative computer vision and continual machine learning for artificial general intelligence. My currently focused machine learning methodologies include generative modelling, continual learning, and language prompting. The ultimate quest of my research is to empower machines with rational, emotional and imaginal intelligence.

Research Description

Visual information is ideally suited for teaching machines with lots of knowledge about the world, because it implicitly encodes the physical constraints that define it. However, existing computer vision models generally approach the visual computing problems with extremely expensive expert knowledge in terms of data pre-processing, manual labeling, feature engineering, neuron design and task assignment. Our research aims at addressing these challenges for both low-level and high-level visual understanding and communication problems such as visual fake, affect and behavior computing. In particular, our research studies automated learning on visual data, label, feature, neuron and task, which aims for making machines to learn the visual world, all by themselves. The PhD student tasks will involve developing novel ideas in the cutting- edge research, as well as conducting data collection for their evaluation.
